* Implications

* Dear friends,

* I am here to soothe and assure you that it is not your fault that you were born white. But while whiteness is not essential to your being, this doesn’t change the fact that you are still white, and will most likely always be white for the rest of your life. Do not forget that. But that also means, since whiteness as a concept is something that exists outside of your self, attacks on whiteness or calls to fight white supremacy are not attacks on your white body.

* You — are under the auspices of the system — and that comes with a certain responsibility. I’m not so eager to say that whiteness is a sin — something you are born into, out of your control, that yet you must be burdened by its history. But I might say that whiteness is a responsibility — you, the one who is so graced by Whiteness Writ Large as being designated as Non-Threatening to Whiteness, have a responsibility to subvert expectations — and be deemed Threatening to Whiteness — to be deemed a Race Traitor. Revoke and reject your whiteness — but know that this is a futile act, at least within our lifetimes. But know that you must try anyways. You must live with this contradiction. An unresolvable contradiction — unless you decenter yourself. Because ultimately, it’s not about any one person. It’s about the whole thing.

* Dear friends,

* I am here to say that the fluctuating and undulating lines of power and identity are so entangled and confused that it is no wonder that you will inevitably get things wrong. I am here to say that it is ok to get things wrong sometimes. I am here to say that maybe you need to accept that you will get things wrong sometimes. It is hard to break free, to dis-align yourself with the establishment. After all, you have a lot to lose.
And it is at this point that I stop really believing in this premise.

Whiteness as hyperobject? Object-oriented ontology? Give me a break. Looking at whiteness as an identity is obviously still a relevant viewpoint and will probably remain relevant for a very long time. The anger, trauma, and blame for centuries-long histories of incredible violence and domination is not so easily sloughed off just because we see whiteness as a system that exists outside of any body.

But I suppose I still believe in revolutionary love. The white man’s burden is really the burden of history. I am making a conscious decision to love everyone. This is not an emotional decision. This is an ideological decision. I choose, despite whatever spur of the moment reactions I may have, to extend people generosity, so that they may extend the same generosity to me. To share with people what I know, so that they may share what they know with me. To give people the benefit of the doubt, so that, when I find myself also somehow aligning myself to hegemony, they may show me the errors of my ways.
I wonder, am I being to alienating?

I don’t want to talk about this anymore. Im bored. Im tired of it. I don’t want to talk about it. Im not interested in being a spectacle. Im not interested in being radical. I’m happy being complicit.
This is not a hill to die on. This is not a worldview to subscribe to.
